 		You can do something similar, that isn't a glitch. It requires tracks, though.
This tutorial of mine written horribly. I hope someone can rewrite this.

1. Place a Question Block (with whatever inside in) on a horizontal rail. Make the rail as long as the amount of Question blocks you want combined.
Let's say 2 quesiton blocks combined. For this, you'd want make it 2 rails long. Place the block on one of those rails.

2. On the other rail, make it drop the Question block. Then, place a third rail off to the the side of the rail that drops. Place it so that the question block moves on the 2nd rail on drops onto the 3rd rail.

3. Place another horizontal rail on top of the first one. On that rail, place a question block with whatever you want inside it. Make it go the same way. Also, make that rail drop the question block.

If you were able to understand this gibberish, then the tutorial should've worked. I call this stacking objects. You could do this with enemies like Boo rings and Bowser.
